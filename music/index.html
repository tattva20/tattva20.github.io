DJ Forza — sixteen years on the decks

From the Psytrance underground to Melodic Techno maturity. The Tattva Music label, a discography across three movements, and a transoceanic collaboration network.

The music life

Music was my first international passport. As DJ Forza I spent sixteen years inside the Mexican and Latin American electronic scene, building sets that ranged from deep psychedelic textures to peak-time techno energy. I played across all of Mexico, throughout Brazil, in India, the United States and Chile, sharing the stage at festivals of the stature of Universo Paralelo. In 2012 I was honored as the Best Techno DJ in Mexico.

Tattva Music — the label, and its quiet mission

I founded and ran Tattva Music, a record label that became a small home for Mexican and international electronic artists I admired. The Sanskrit name was not decorative — it was a promise. Tattva means "the essence of things," and the label emerged with a specific intention:

"To foster unity and mutual understanding between the world's different cultures through music — and to honor the cultures of the peoples who inhabit this earth."

Curating a label, in that spirit, became an act of hospitality across continents — building bridges between Mexican, Israeli, Brazilian, Japanese, Bulgarian, German and British voices, so that they could sound, together, bigger than they would alone. The roster grew to include artists such as Ben Coda, Stan Kolev, Paul Thomas, Weekend Heroes, Ben Weber and the Japanese producer Yuji Ono, with whom we drew a quiet cultural bridge between Mexico and Japan.

Pixan — Trance from the New World

In 2004 I compiled Pixan — Trance from the New World for the Austrian imprint Synergetic Records, a journey through the psychedelic and progressive trance of the Americas. The compilation was warmly received internationally and was highly acclaimed by Mushroom Magazine in 2005 — a recognition I still treasure, because it placed a Mexican curatorial voice on the global trance map.

Teaching the craft

For years I also taught what I loved: DJ technique, music production, mixing and mastering. Passing on the craft is one of the most rewarding parts of any art — it keeps the discipline humble and the imagination open.

"Me Encanta Dios"

My most recognized track was born during a personal pilgrimage. I have always been deeply drawn to the philosophies of India; for a few years I lived a mystical, contemplative chapter that took me from Ranchi to Rishikesh, from chanting to silence. That inner journey became sound, and the result was a piece that many people still carry with affection: Me Encanta Dios.

Elite collaborations — global imprints

Beyond the home of Tattva Music, my work crossed over to some of the biggest names in the global electronic industry. Tracks of mine were licensed and released on imprints like 1605 — the legendary label of Slovenian DJ UMEK, one of techno's most respected voices — Outta Limits (Stan Kolev's Bulgarian imprint) and Undergroove Records, among others. To see my name in the catalogues of labels whose releases I had bought and worshipped as a young DJ was — and still is — a quiet, immense honor.

I closed that musical chapter in 2016 with gratitude and without regret. The decks are quiet for now — but the rhythm never leaves the body.

Discography — three movements (2004 – 2016)

The music life as DJ Forza unfolded in three distinct movements, each one a deliberate evolution of the technical signature and the philosophy beneath the sound. Every release below is verifiable on Beatport.

Movement I — Psychedelic Climax (2004 – 2007)

The era of expansive open-air sound, rolling basslines, and mental progressions designed for daylight peaks.

  • "Me Encanta Dios" · 2004–2005 · Tremor Records / Synergetic Records · the foundational track of the international career.
  • Pixan — Trance from the New World · 2004 · compiled by DJ Forza for Synergetic Records · acclaimed by Mushroom Magazine in 2005.
  • "Karma Strikers" + "Energy Surf" · 2006 · Sonesta Records · with Lior Lahav (Israel).

Movement II — Brazilian Underground Transition (2008 – 2011)

After moving to Brazil, the BPM dropped from 140 to 125–128, and the sound mutated from psychedelia into minimal, hypnotic, structurally precise Tech House and Minimal Techno.

  • "Supersprite" · 2008 · Sounds of Earth · the first major formal step into the new sound.
  • "Bone Groove" EP · 2009 · Sounds of Earth · the core of the Minimal Techno chapter, beloved by DJs for its dry percussion and ultra-clean sub-bass.
  • "Minimalia" EP · 2009 · Sounds of Earth · continuation of the minimal language.
  • "Me Encanta Dios (2011 Remix)" + "Terra Sul" · 2011 · Sounds of Earth · with Thiago Merli (Brazil) — the original mystical piece reimagined under a sober Groove Techno frame.

Movement III — Tattva Maturity (2013 – 2016)

With the founding of Tattva Music, the focus settled on Melodic Techno, sophisticated Progressive House and experimental broken rhythms — elegant, cinematic, high-fidelity productions.

  • "Dia Cero" · 2013 · Tattva Music · the inaugural track that defined the mature language of the label.
  • "Far East (Forza Remix)" + "Tonight You Are Mine (Forza Remix)" · 2014–2015 · Tattva Music · remixes for Yuji Ono — the transoceanic bridge between Mexico and Japan made audible.
  • "Disco Duro" · 2016 · Outta Limits (Stan Kolev's label, Bulgaria) · the closing track of the active musical chapter — a robotic, industrial, mathematically danceable homage, by title and by sound, to the engineering chapter that was just beginning.

"I do not use presets. I code my own oscillators."
